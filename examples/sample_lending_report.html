Executive summary

**BLUF:** Credit risk is concentrating in identifiable, controllable segments—Grade E loans (up to 56.3% default), small-business loans (35.55%), and weak-credit borrowers (FICO<660 or DTI>30 default at 35.18%)—all running roughly 1.4x–2x the recompute-confirmed overall rate of 25.22%, signaling that current underwriting standards are too loose. The strongest drivers are consistent across analyses: grade (18.6%–50.9% range), interest rate and DTI (r=+0.16 each), and FICO (r=−0.14), with default rates climbing monotonically as credit quality worsens. Default appears to be rising over time (23.0%→27.6% earlier-to-recent), driven mainly by within-segment deterioration concentrated in Grade B rather than mix shift, though this trend assumes a linear pattern, may be affected by right-censoring, and recent-period outcomes may be immature and should not be over-read. Our best model (LogisticRegression, held-out ROC-AUC=0.703, CV 0.693) supports these drivers but comes from a single held-out split not yet validated over time or on new data, so it should be treated as a directional decision aid pending further validation. **Overall confidence: high** for the historical/segment findings (grounded and recompute-confirmed) and medium for forward-looking trend and model-generalization claims.
